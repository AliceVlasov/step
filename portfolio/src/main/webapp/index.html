PROJECTS

(Obviously) I am currently a STEP intern at Google, and this website is one of our projects for this summer.

This year I joined a student satellite development team at the University of Edinburgh called Asteria as a software engineer. The goal of the project is to design and build a nanosatellite which will be launched into Low-Earth-Orbit for a few years to collect atmospheric data that will be used for research.

In March of this year, I was one of the organizers of the Hoppers International Women's Day conference which featured workshops, various speakers, and activities, and next year, will be part of the Hoppers committee as a Big Events coordinator.

During Programming Club this year, I implemented a stochastic, policy-based, Monte Carlo algorithm to teach an agent how to balance a pole on a cart moving in 2D.

Last summer, I started to learn about Machine Learning on my own and, in order to better understand how it works, designed and implemented a generic learning environment for a computer to play any game that can be divided in to States, Actions, and Agents. I then implemented a Reinforcement Learning algorithm and experimented with hyperparameter optimization to teach the computer agent to master any game it is presented with as fast as possible.

Two summers ago, I learned about steganographic algorithms for the first time so I decided to try it myself. This project entailed designing and implementing a steganographic algorithm that is cabable of encrypting one image into another by overwriting the least significant bigs of the RGB values of the visible images with the bits representing the RGB values of the hidden image.

In grades 11 and 12 I was an organizer of the first and second annual Girls in Tech Conference, in Toronto. The conference attracted around 100 participants both years, and included panels of women in a variety of technology-related fields, several workshops covering topics from robotics to programming, as well as encryption and 3D design, and presenters ranging from hichschool students to representatives of Google, IBM, and even local organizations.

My first coding project was to design and implement a classic game of Tetris using keyboard-controlled graphics and object-oriented design.

In 2014, I had the opportunity to participate in the Student Spaceflight Experiment Program (and watch a SpaceX Falcon 9 launch as well). My team was selected to design an experiment that was executed by astronauts on the ISS, after which we then publish a paper with our findings.

EDUCATION

From grades 7 to 12, I commuted to downtown Toronto to attend the University of Toronto Schools (not to be confused with the University of Toronto). During my last few years of highschool, I took mostly science and math classes, but only took my first coding class in grade 10 which I loved, so I decided to participate in coding competitions, become an executive for the school's coding club, and study CS in university.

I am currently attending the University of Edinburgh in Scotland and I have finished my first year of my degree in AI and Computer Science. Outside of classes I also participated in the School of Informatics' coding club and was an active member of Hoppers, the women in informatics society at the unversity. I had a great time in my first year and am looking forward to the years to come!

Over the summer last year I was accepted to the Quantum Cryptography School for Young Students at the University of Waterloo. Over the course of two weeks, I, along with 44 other participants from across the world, learned the basics of quantum mechanics, quantum computing, and quantum cryptography.
